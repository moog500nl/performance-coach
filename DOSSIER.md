### Recovery Protocol

**Recovery Triggers (Auto-Deload):**
- HRV ↓ >20% from 7-day baseline → No quality work; Z2 only or full rest. Resume normally if HRV recovers next day.
- RHR ↑ ≥5 bpm from baseline → Drop to Z2 or full rest. RHR elevation often lags stress by a day — treat as early warning.
- Feel ≥ 4 → Full rest or short easy walk only. No Zwift. At 52, pushing through a feel-4 day costs more than it returns.
- Two+ triggers simultaneously → Complete rest. Non-negotiable. Signals systemic fatigue, illness or accumulated stress.

**Additional note (from July):** Loaded walking days (6–8kg pack, 2hrs+) count as training stress and should be factored into next-morning readiness assessment. Elevated RHR or feel-3+ the morning after a long walk warrants a lighter Zwift session or rest.

**Feel Scale:**
| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Excellent (fully recovered) |
| 2 | Good (normal fatigue) |
| 3 | Moderate (manageable tiredness) |
| 4 | Fatigued (reduced readiness, deload trigger) |
| 5 | Exhausted (complete rest required) |

## 9. Coach Notes

**Event context:** MDS Trek is a multi-day trekking event, not a cycling race. Zwift is the primary tool for building the aerobic engine and managing weight. Each stage (max 19km / ~3.5hrs at target pace) is individually well within aerobic capacity — the challenge is cumulative fatigue across 5 consecutive days and load-bearing stress from the pack.

**Key principles:**
- Z2 aerobic volume is the most transferable adaptation from cycling to trekking
- Back-to-back Zwift sessions teach multi-day recovery — schedule these deliberately
- Weight loss is the single highest-impact variable: 97kg → 88–90kg by October significantly reduces mechanical demand over 75km
- Loaded walking (6–8kg pack) must begin by July — foot, ankle, hip and lower back conditioning cannot be faked with cycling
- Foot care is critical: blisters are the primary DNF cause in multi-day treks; test socks, shoes and anti-chafe protocol before the event
